# ✈️ Design, Build, and Testing of a Transonic Wing Assembly

## 📖 Overview
A comprehensive lifecycle mechanical engineering project focusing on the aerodynamic design, rapid prototyping, and physical validation of a transonic wing. This project demonstrates the ability to translate complex aerodynamic requirements into a physical test article while navigating strict manufacturing constraints and tolerances.

## 🛠️ Key Engineering Contributions
**CAD Modeling**: Modeled a complex 3D wing structure in SolidWorks by lofting two distinct transonic airfoils at the root and tip, engineered with a precise 0.2-meter chord length.

**Material & Structural Analysis**: Evaluated material density, manufacturing cost, and structural integrity to balance aerodynamic performance with the physical realities of rapid prototyping.

**Design for Manufacturing (DFM)**: Engineered a robust dovetail joint to physically assemble the scaled model, overcoming the volumetric size constraints of the available 3D printers.

**Sensor Integration**: Integrated a custom array of 10 surface pressure taps directly into the CAD geometry to facilitate high-resolution aerodynamic data acquisition.

**Prototyping & Iteration**: Fabricated and iterated upon an initial physical prototype to validate assembly tolerances and refine the structural model prior to final printing.

**Physical Testing**: Executed subsonic wind tunnel testing across a range of velocities, targeting operational flight speeds of 5 to 25
 m/s, to experimentally capture the divergence Mach number and pinpoint boundary layer separation.

**Data Processing**: Processed raw wind tunnel data by converting physical pressure tap readings into force units, verifying the initial aerodynamic calculations against real-world performance.

**Documentation**: Authored a comprehensive technical report detailing the design methodology, manufacturing challenges, and experimental findings.

## 🧰 Tools & Technologies

***Software***: SolidWorks, Python (NumPy, Matplotlib), Excel, Airfoiltools, XFoil

***Hardware/Testing***: Subsonic Wind Tunnel, 3D Printers, Pressure Transducers, Assembly parts/tools
